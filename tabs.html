What are Product Limits?

Product limits are the minimum and maximum quantity for individual product/variant items that your customers may add to their cart. You have two methods to define product limits for each individual item you offer: Add a product or SKU (variant) from a database of your offerings or batch upload by CSV. Please find the buttons located at bottom of this page to perform the list updating of your choice. If you offer an item that is available in different variants (i.e. widgets in blue, red, and green), you can check the "Combine Variants" box to apply limits across all the individual item's variants.

Product

  • Min Minimal quantity for this product/SKU required per shopping cart. Entering any value here wouldn't make this item mandatory though. If the customer doesn't add it into their cart, the minimum wouldn't get triggered.
  • Max Maximal quantity for this product/SKU allowed per shopping cart. Entering 0 would be treated as if the maximum is unset. To disallow product's purchasing please enter any value between 0 and 1 (0.5 for instance), or (better) hide this product from your online store using the standard Shopify admin interface instead.
  • Multiple Is only useful when you need to sell something in certain multiples. Example: for soda 6-packs only (6, 12, 18, etc.) enter 6 below.
  • Combine Variants If this checkbox is turned ON, then all this (and only this) product's variants found in the cart would be limited as a total quantity. Otherwise, if it's turned OFF, then each variant/SKU would be required to meet those per-product Min, Max or Multiple individually.

What are Group Limits?

Group limits are limit rules you set that apply across more than one item. You can categorize your items based on a common keyword in product titles, types, handles, variant titles, SKU, collection, tag or by similar price. Please read more tips in this article.
